TODAY'S OPPORTUNITY

Building On Top Of The New Custom-Silicon Stack

With OpenAI, Google, Apple, and Meta all racing to build their own chips, inference is about to get cheaper across the board. The opportunity isn't competing with the labs — it's building tooling, benchmarking, and migration services that help smaller companies take advantage of cheaper inference as it rolls out.

Why it matters: Every major lab is now treating custom chips as a strategic moat, not just a cost play. As inference costs fall, the businesses that win will be the ones already positioned to pass those savings on.

Latest News

AI

OpenAI And Broadcom Unveil Jalapeño Chip

OpenAI revealed Jalapeño, its first custom inference chip built with Broadcom, designed specifically to run trained models for ChatGPT, Codex, and API traffic rather than training them. The goal is cutting the cost of serving AI at scale as usage keeps climbing.

SECURITY

Anthropic Accuses Alibaba Of Mass Distillation Attack

Anthropic told US senators that roughly 25,000 fraudulent accounts ran 28.8 million exchanges with Claude over six weeks to extract its agentic reasoning and coding abilities. No systems were breached — the accounts simply used Claude like ordinary users at massive scale.

FUNDING

Sierra Raises $950M, Tops $15B Valuation

Bret Taylor's enterprise AI agent startup Sierra closed a $950 million round led by Tiger Global and GV. The company says it now serves over 40% of the Fortune 50, with agents handling everything from mortgage refinancing to insurance claims and returns processing.

MARKET

ChatGPT's Market Share Slips Below Half

A widely cited 2026 industry report found ChatGPT's share of the AI assistant market fell to 46.4%, with Gemini climbing to 27.7% and Claude reaching 10.3%. Claude still leads on paid conversion rate, suggesting users are actively comparing assistants rather than defaulting to one.

STARTUP

SpaceX Acquires Cursor-Maker Anysphere For $60B

SpaceX announced an all-stock acquisition of Anysphere, the company behind the Cursor coding assistant, in one of the AI industry's biggest deals to date. The move signals coding tools are becoming core infrastructure, not just a standalone product category.

TREND

Custom AI Chips Are Becoming The New Competitive Edge

Google, Apple, Meta, and now OpenAI are all building custom silicon to cut their dependence on Nvidia. The shift signals that AI infrastructure is turning into a strategic moat rather than just a line item on the cloud bill.

OPPORTUNITY

Smaller Open-Source Models Are Closing The Gap

Compact open-source models are increasingly competitive with general-purpose giants on legal, healthcare, and engineering tasks, at a fraction of the cost. That's opening real space for niche builders to compete without frontier-scale budgets.

SECURITY

"Agentjacking" Attack Hits Coding Agents Hard

A new attack class disclosed this month tricks coding agents like Claude Code and Cursor into executing malicious commands hidden inside fake error reports. It's already affected thousands of organizations — a reminder to treat tool output as untrusted input before acting on it.

FUNDING

AI Funding Concentrates In Fewer, Bigger Rounds

Series A rounds for AI startups now average over $50 million, roughly 30% above non-AI peers. Investors are increasingly backing proof over hype — pilots and a sharp problem at seed, paying customers and retention by Series A.

AI

Qwen Unveils "AgentWorld" Simulated Training Approach

Alibaba's Qwen team introduced a model that simulates seven different agent environments at once, learning to model the environment itself rather than just operate inside it — a step toward agents that train faster using simulation instead of real-world interaction.

OPPORTUNITY

Context Engineering Is Replacing Prompt Engineering

As agentic workflows get longer and more complex, the real skill gap is shifting from writing good prompts to building the full information environment that keeps an agent reliable across many steps. Builders who get ahead of this will have an edge.

IDEA

AI Stack For Solo Operators Under $500/Month

Founders are replacing $80K-plus monthly payroll with agent stacks for coding, content, support, design, and automation — all under $500 a month. The one-person company is no longer a constraint; it is a strategy.

TRENDING

3 Things Builders Should Watch This Week

- OpenAI entering custom silicon with Jalapeño — inference costs are about to drop
- Anthropic vs. Alibaba distillation dispute raising questions about model access and abuse
- SpaceX's $60B Cursor acquisition signaling coding tools are becoming core AI infrastructure
